Aaron Chalmers began his combat sports career in professional MMA in the Londo- based promotion BAMMA. He later went over to Bellator and had mixed results at that higher level with an overall record of 5-2. To dip his toes into the world of striking only competitions, he boxed a few times, including a match against the UFC’s Kiefer Crosbie and then took off the gloves to fight for BKFC.

The first time he toed the line it was against fellow Brit, Chas Symonds where he showcased a dominant performance, dropping his opponent twice before the eventual TKO in round 3. With a high volume, come forward approach Chalmers is looking to entertain and get a finish.

Chalmers is a proud father with a tight knit circle of friends who he trains with and enjoys life outside of fighting with. Chalmers second fight in the Squared Circle will come against the BKFC debutant, Jack Fincham at BKFC 81 in Manchester.

Who we are

Bare Knuckle Fighting Championship (BKFC) is the first promotion in the U.S. since 1889 to hold legal and regulated bare knuckle events. Based in Philadelphia and led by President David Feldman, BKFC focuses on preserving the legacy of bare knuckle fighting with a specifically created rule that emphasizes fighter safety.
